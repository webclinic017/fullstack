Will you close any of my positions?

When the margin ratio (margin/free margin) is below 50%, TigerWit will effect liquidation starting from your position showing the highest loss. Please maintain sufficient margin on your account at all times

What is a price re-quotation and when might it happen?

The most common reasons for a price re-quotation are:
  1. • Volatility: When markets are experiencing high volatility, the price may change dramatically.
  2. • Slow connectivity: You may be trading on a poor internet connection which can result in price latency.
In cases such as these we may offer a revised market quote to you. You can choose to accept the re-quoted price (which may itself then require a re-quote) or cancel the order.

What is slippage and when might it occur?

In case of liquidation of a position, or when a stop-loss is triggered, the trade is executed at the actual market price. Immediately following a stop-loss or liquidation being triggered, the actual market price may no longer be at the trigger level. In the case of gaps, the trade will be made at the first available market price following the gap, which may differ from the previously quoted price.

What is the minimum lot size I can trade?

The minimum lot size is 0.01 Lot for FX and precious metals and 0.1 Lot for indices and oils

How can I view the details of the trading products?

On the app, click on the ‘?’ icon at the top of the trading symbol to view product details.

What leverage do you offer?

TigerWit offers leverage 200 : 1 depending on the market, although Crypto Currency (Bitcoin etc) leverage is 2 :1. Higher leverage may be available if you are eligible to be treated as a Professional Client. Please contact Client Support for more information at customerservice@tigerwit.com.

What trading fees do you charge?

TigerWit only charges the spread. There is no commission applicable.

Is there a time limit on holding open positions or orders to open?

There is no time limit for open orders or positions. There is no limit on the number of orders or positions you can have open at any time so long as you maintain sufficient margin to cover any losses and swap charges.
